<p><p>The smoked eel market is categorized primarily into hot-smoked and cold-smoked segments. Hot-smoked eel is cooked and infused with smoke simultaneously, resulting in a moist, flaky texture and a strong smoky flavor, typically found in ready-to-eat products. In contrast, cold-smoked eel undergoes a smoking process without cooking, preserving its delicate texture and subtle taste, often used in gourmet dishes. Both market types cater to varying culinary preferences and are popular in gourmet food circles and seafood delicacies.</p></p>
